Amanda Marquez

Dance Like Everyone is Watching

ABOUT

Amanda is a versatile Performer, Instructor and Choreographer in multiple dance styles including Jazz, Contemporary, Ballet and Latin Dance. She specializes in Latin Jazz and NY Style Salsa On 2, or Mambo. Her dance training began with classical ballet, musical theater and modern dance at the school of the Garden State Ballet in NJ and continued throughout the years in Los Angeles, San Francisco and NYC.

While pursuing her career in NYC, Amanda taught at some of the most prestigious dance institutions including The Joffrey Ballet School, Ballet Hispanico, Alvin Ailey, Broadway Dance Center and Peridance Capezio Center. She was also the Primary Dance Specialist at Columbia Grammar and Preparatory School in Manhattan, a Choreographer for Central Park Dance Academy in Scarsdale, and was on staff at The College of New Rochelle where she taught Latin Dance. Currently, she teaches for both Castro Valley Performing Arts Center and Dance Attack Performing Arts here in the Bay Area. Recently, she founded ENCANTO Youth Latin Dance Company and Proyecto Mambo Adult Training Company, for which she also acts as Artistic Director.

In 2012, Amanda signed with MSA Talent Agency in NY and worked closely with latin choreographers Sekou McMiller and Maria Torres. Performance credits include Broadway Bares Equity Fights Aids at the Hammerstein Ballroom, The Latin Billboard Awards, where she danced for Popstar Don Omar, the New York Knicks Latin Night at Madison Square Garden, the 33rd New York Salsa Festival at the Barclays Center, featuring salsa legends Willie Colon, Grupo Niche, Eddie Palmieri, Frankie Negron, Eddie Santiago and Jerry Rivera, multiple benefits for the Latino Commission Against Aids, the Latin Nutcracker, television appearances on Telemundo, Univision, The BBC Vancouver and The Fox Upfronts featuring Terry Bradshaw and Regis Philbin at the Marquis Theater. Amanda has also traveled as a performer and instructor throughout the US and internationally.

Amanda is also a certified Yoga Instructor through Greenpath Yoga in San Francisco and holds her BA in Sociology with a concentration in Family Studies from San Jose State University.

"I believe that a deep understanding of the body's mechanics and movement pathways is the starting point for all dancers. Only from this awareness can we express motion in its truest form. Train the body correctly, and we can be fully authentic in our pursuit of beauty."
